Safety & Wellbeing

Safety isn't a feature. It's the starting point.

Every guardianship service says "your child is safe." We'd rather show you what that actually looks like.

Peace of Mind for Families

Knowing your child has a trusted safety network nearby changes everything. You stop wondering and start sleeping.

Through our partnership with Community Patrol, Crestway Global gives students immediate access to help when they need it. That means you and your child can focus on what matters: grades, growth, and actually enjoying the experience.

  • We visit housing before anything gets signed. Smoke detectors, landlord background checks, neighborhood verification.
  • 24/7 emergency line answered by someone who knows your student's name. Not a call center.
  • Airport pickup, first-week city orientation, and a clear plan for when something goes wrong.
  • Crestway Assist: a 24/7 safety button inside the app. One tap connects your child to someone who already knows their situation.

Personal Safety & Assistance

New country. Unfamiliar situations. That's normal. What shouldn't be normal is having no one to call.

Through our partnership with Community Patrol, you can access priority assistance through the Crestway app. If you need guidance or someone to step in, help is one notification away.

  • We check your housing before you move in. Fire safety, landlord verification, neighborhood walkthrough.
  • 24/7 line answered by someone who knows your name and your situation.
  • First-week city orientation so you know where the clinic is, how transit works, and what to do if something goes sideways.
  • Crestway Assist: a safety button in the app. One tap. Someone who knows you responds.

Admissions

We accept fewer students than we turn away.

Personalized support requires a low ratio of students to staff. If we accepted everyone, we'd become what we're not: a checkbox guardianship service that loses track of individuals.

What we're looking for

  • Students who want to grow, not just survive.
  • Consistent effort (your transcript doesn't need to be perfect).
  • Willingness to engage with a new community.
  • Parents who want to stay involved without hovering.

What we're not looking for

  • Students who need 24/7 supervision (we're support, not surveillance).
  • Families expecting us to replace parenting entirely.

FAQ

The questions parents actually ask.

What happens if my child has an emergency at 2am?

Students contact our emergency line, answered by an assigned advisor familiar with their situation. We coordinate hospital visits, housing access issues, travel complications, and other urgent matters. Families receive notification once the situation is addressed.

How is this different from the guardianship my kid's school requires?

Most school-required guardianship services are paperwork. We're involved week to week: sharing context, guiding decisions, and helping families navigate local systems.

My kid wants independence. Won't this feel like babysitting?

Our students aren't being watched. They're being supported. When they need help, they don't have to figure it out alone.
