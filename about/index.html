About Us

The trusted companion for students studying away from home.

Professional guardianship combining institutional expertise with culturally-informed support for international families.

Our Mission

Ensuring safety, comfort, and personal growth through culturally-aware support.

Brightway was established to address a documented gap: Canadian universities provide essential academic and administrative support, but lack the resources for ongoing, personalized welfare monitoring that international families expect.

Our approach combines professional expertise with cultural understanding. We maintain structured protocols for safety, communication, and accountability—while recognizing that effective guardianship requires human connection and cultural fluency.

Leadership

Combined expertise in public safety and institutional development

Brightway's founders bring complementary backgrounds: decades of law enforcement and community safety experience, alongside real estate development and student housing operations.

Dave at age 20, early in his police career

Dave Murray

Co-Founder & Director of Operations

Dave Murray served the City of Toronto for 31 years as a police officer, retiring at the rank of Staff Sergeant. His career included seven years as a criminal investigator, developing expertise in safety assessment, risk management, and community protection protocols.

This background directly informs Brightway's safeguarding procedures. Dave established our housing verification protocols, emergency response systems, and staff training programs. His institutional knowledge of Toronto's support systems—healthcare, law enforcement, social services—enables Brightway to navigate complex situations effectively.

Dave leads operations with an emphasis on documented procedures, accountability, and preventive care. His approach ensures that Brightway's support is both personally attentive and professionally structured.

Doug Hochglaube

Co-Founder & Strategic Director

Doug Hochglaube is a real estate developer with nearly two decades of experience in student housing and community-focused projects across Ontario. His work has involved the development and management of housing for thousands of students, giving him direct insight into the gaps between institutional support and student needs.

This operational background shapes Brightway's structural approach. Doug developed our partnership frameworks with universities, our housing assessment protocols, and our scalability planning. His experience with regulatory compliance, insurance, and risk management informs our governance standards.

Doug directs Brightway's institutional relationships and strategic development, ensuring that our growth maintains service quality and professional standards. His focus is on building sustainable systems that can deliver consistent, accountable support at scale.

What Guides Us

Support without surveillance. Safety without fear.

Proactive care

We don't wait for crises. Regular check-ins and local presence mean we catch issues early, before they become emergencies.

Cultural fluency

Our team speaks Mandarin and understands the cultural context Chinese students come from. We bridge two worlds.

Genuine connection

Students aren't case numbers. We know their names, their goals, and what keeps their parents up at night.

Transparent communication

Parents receive regular, specific updates. No vague reassurances—real information about how their child is doing.
